Featured Work

Genome Biology Bioconductor R package

xCell 2.0: cell-type enrichment that predicts immunotherapy response

A next-generation tool that turns bulk transcriptomes into cell-type proportions using ontology-aware modeling, improved signatures, and spillover correction. It outperforms eleven existing deconvolution methods and predicts patient response to immune checkpoint blockade. Released as an open-source Bioconductor R package.

Paper of the Month, Technion Faculty of Biology, September 2025

Paper Code Bioconductor Talk: ILANIT 2023

bioRxiv preprint Clinical prediction model

Predicting immunotherapy response from chemotherapy-induced tumor remodeling

Chemotherapy before immunotherapy helps some patients but not others, and no biomarker could identify them in advance. From 1,936 immunotherapy-treated patients and 334 paired pre/post-chemotherapy biopsies, this work derives a 50-gene chemo-conversion signature that flags likely responders before treatment. It validated in held-out clinical cohorts (IMvigor210 bladder cancer, AUC 0.80; NSCLC replication, AUC 0.73) and showed no signal without preceding chemotherapy, opening a path to precision sequencing of chemotherapy and immunotherapy.
